Anne Frank

Anne Frank was born in the Germany city of Frankfurt am Main in 1929. She was the daughter of Otto and Edith Frank. On September 1, 1939, when Anne was 10 years old, Nazi Germany invaded Poland, and the Second World War began. In the spring of 1942, Anne's father had started furnishing a hiding place in the annex of his business premises at Prinsengracht 263. On Anne's 13th birthday, before they went into hiding, she was presented with a diary. She wrote about events in the Secret Annex, but also about her feelings and thoughts. She wrote short stories, started on a novel and copied passages fromm the books she read. She and the other people hiding in the annex were discovered and arrested by police officers on August 4, 1944. Anne, Margot, and their mother were sent to the labour camp for women. In early November 1944, Anne was put on trasport and was deported to the Bergen-Belsen concentration camp with Margot. Their parents were left behind in Auschwitz. The conditions in Bergen-Belsen were horrible and because of the lack of food, cold and contagious diseases, Anne and Margot contacted typhus. In February 1945, ,they both died owning to its effects.

Legacy

Known for her diary that recounted life in hiding during the holocaust, Anne Frank has become a potent symbol of the Nazi genocide that killed six million Jews and about five million non-Jews between 1939 and 1945.
